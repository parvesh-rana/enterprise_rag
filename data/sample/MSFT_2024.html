Microsoft Corporation

Annual Report on Form 10-K for the fiscal year ended June 30, 2024.

Item 1. Business

Microsoft Corporation is a technology company whose mission is to empower every person and every organization on the planet to achieve more. The Company develops and supports software, services, devices, and solutions. The Company's products include operating systems, cross-device productivity and collaboration applications, server applications, business solution applications, desktop and server management tools, software development tools, and video games. The Company also designs and sells devices and offers an array of services, including cloud-based solutions that provide customers with software, services, platforms, and content.

The Company operates in three segments: Productivity and Business Processes, Intelligent Cloud, and More Personal Computing. The Productivity and Business Processes segment consists of products and services in the portfolio of productivity, communication, and information services, spanning a variety of devices and platforms. This segment primarily comprises Office Commercial, Office Consumer, LinkedIn, and Dynamics products and cloud services.

The Intelligent Cloud segment consists of the Company's public, private, and hybrid server products and cloud services that can power modern business and developers. This segment primarily comprises Server products and cloud services, including Azure and other cloud services, SQL Server, Windows Server, Visual Studio, System Center, and related Client Access Licenses, Enterprise Mobility + Security, and GitHub.

Azure is a comprehensive set of cloud services that offers developers and IT professionals the ability to build, deploy, and manage applications through a global network of datacenters. Azure services include Azure AI services, which provide pre-built and customizable AI models and APIs. Azure OpenAI Service allows customers to access OpenAI's powerful language models, including GPT-4 and DALL-E, from the Azure cloud with enterprise security, compliance, and regional availability.

The More Personal Computing segment consists of products and services that put customers at the center of the experience with the Company's technology. This segment primarily comprises Windows, including Windows OEM licensing and other non-volume licensing of the Windows operating system, Windows Commercial, Devices including Surface and PC accessories, Gaming including Xbox hardware and content and services, and Search and news advertising.

Competition: The Company faces intense competition across all markets for its products and services. The rapid pace of technological change continually creates new opportunities for competitors. Key competitive factors include security of products and services, privacy, product features, quality and performance, the ability to integrate with other services and platforms, and total cost of ownership.

Research and Development: The Company invests heavily in research and development. Research and development expense was $29.5 billion in fiscal 2024. The Company's research and development efforts include artificial intelligence, cloud engineering, gaming, and emerging technologies.

Employees: As of June 30, 2024, Microsoft employed approximately 228,000 people on a full-time basis. The Company considers its relations with its employees to be good.

Item 1A. Risk Factors

Our operations and financial results are subject to various risks and uncertainties, including those described below, that could adversely affect our business, financial condition, results of operations, cash flows, and the trading price of our common stock.

Evolving business model: An important element of our business model involves investing in areas such as cloud-based services and artificial intelligence. Our increasing focus on cloud-based services presents execution and competitive risks. The success of our cloud and AI investments depends on our ability to deliver compelling services at competitive prices while managing costs effectively.

Cybersecurity and data privacy risks: Cyberattacks and security vulnerabilities could lead to reduced revenue, increased costs, liability claims, or harm to our competitive position. Maintaining the security of our products, services, and our internal data is critical to customer trust. Data handling practices and compliance with evolving privacy regulations may expose us to additional risks.

Competition: We face intense competition from companies such as Amazon, Apple, Google, and others. Our competitors range from large international companies with significant resources to small, specialized firms. The technology sector is characterized by rapid technological change, frequent product introductions, and evolving industry standards.

AI risks: We are building AI into many of our offerings, and as AI continues to evolve, the development and use of AI raises complex ethical, legal, and technical challenges. Responsible AI development, bias mitigation, and reliability are critical to maintaining trust in our AI-powered products and services.

Regulatory environment: We face regulatory risks globally. The European Union's Digital Markets Act, antitrust investigations, and data protection regulations such as GDPR may impact our business. Changes in tax laws or unfavorable resolution of tax examinations could adversely affect our tax provision or tax rate.

Item 1C. Cybersecurity

Microsoft has comprehensive cybersecurity risk management processes. Our security strategy is built on zero-trust principles. We employ a defense-in-depth approach that includes threat intelligence, vulnerability management, security monitoring, and incident response. Our Chief Information Security Officer oversees our cybersecurity organization and reports to senior management and the Board of Directors.

Item 2. Properties

Our corporate headquarters is located in Redmond, Washington. We have approximately 42 million square feet of space for use by all segments. We own and lease datacenters globally to support our cloud services. Significant facilities are located in the Puget Sound area, Silicon Valley, and various international locations.

Item 3. Legal Proceedings

Microsoft is a defendant in various legal proceedings and claims. The Company does not believe that any current legal proceedings will have a material adverse effect on its financial statements, although the outcome of litigation is inherently uncertain.

European Commission and Regulatory Investigations: The European Commission continues to review certain of the Company's business practices under the Digital Markets Act. In addition, various regulatory authorities around the world have initiated investigations into certain aspects of the Company's AI and cloud computing practices.

Item 7. Management's Discussion and Analysis of Financial Condition and Results of Operations

Revenue: Total revenue was $245.1 billion, an increase of 16% compared to fiscal 2023. Revenue from Intelligent Cloud was $105.4 billion, up 21% year-over-year, driven by Azure and other cloud services growth of 30%. Productivity and Business Processes revenue was $80.8 billion, up 13%. More Personal Computing revenue was $58.9 billion, up 9%.

Azure and Cloud Services: Azure and other cloud services revenue grew 30% in fiscal 2024, driven by consumption-based services growth and increasing AI services adoption. The growth reflected demand for cloud migration, digital transformation initiatives, and AI workloads powered by Azure OpenAI Service.

Operating Income: Operating income was $109.4 billion, an increase of 24% compared to fiscal 2023. Operating margin expanded to 44.6% from 41.8%. The improvement was primarily driven by revenue growth exceeding operating expense growth, favorable mix shifts toward higher-margin cloud services, and operational efficiencies.

Net Income: Net income was $88.1 billion, an increase of 22% compared to fiscal 2023. Diluted earnings per share was $11.80, an increase of 22%.

Capital Expenditures: Capital expenditures including finance leases were $55.7 billion in fiscal 2024, primarily to support cloud and AI infrastructure growth. We expect capital expenditures to continue to increase as we invest in our cloud and AI capabilities.

Liquidity and Capital Resources: Cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments totaled $75.5 billion as of June 30, 2024. The Company returned $34.0 billion to shareholders through share repurchases and dividends in fiscal 2024.
