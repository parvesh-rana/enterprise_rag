Apple Inc.

Annual Report on Form 10-K for the fiscal year ended September 28, 2024.

Item 1. Business

Apple Inc. designs, manufactures and markets smartphones, personal computers, tablets, wearables and accessories, and sells a variety of related services. The Company's fiscal year is the 52- or 53-week period that ends on the last Saturday of September.

Products: The Company's products include iPhone, Mac, iPad, and Wearables, Home and Accessories. iPhone is the Company's line of smartphones based on its iOS operating system. Mac is the Company's line of personal computers based on its macOS operating system. iPad is the Company's line of multi-purpose tablets based on its iPadOS operating system. Wearables, Home and Accessories includes AirPods, Apple TV, Apple Watch, Beats products, HomePod, and accessories.

Services: The Company's service offerings include advertising, AppleCare, cloud services, digital content and payment services. Digital content stores and streaming services include the App Store, Apple Arcade, Apple Fitness+, Apple Music, Apple News+, Apple TV+, and Apple Books. Payment services include Apple Card and Apple Pay.

Markets and Distribution: The Company's customers are primarily in the consumer, small and mid-sized business, education, enterprise and government markets. The Company sells its products and resells third-party products in most of its major markets directly to consumers, small and mid-sized businesses, and education, enterprise and government customers through its retail and online stores and its direct sales force.

Competition: The markets for the Company's products and services are highly competitive, and are characterized by aggressive price competition and resulting downward pressure on gross margins, frequent introduction of new products and services, short product life cycles, evolving industry standards, continual improvement in product price and performance characteristics, rapid adoption of technological advancements by competitors, and price sensitivity on the part of consumers and businesses.

The Company's ability to compete successfully depends heavily on ensuring the continuing and timely introduction of innovative new products, services and technologies to the marketplace. The Company designs and develops nearly the entire solution for its products, including the hardware, operating system, numerous software applications and related services. Principal competitive factors important to the Company include price, product and service features (including security features), relative price and performance, product and service quality and reliability, design innovation, a strong third-party software and accessories ecosystem, marketing and distribution capability, service and support, and corporate reputation.

Supply Chain: The Company depends on component and product manufacturing and logistical services provided by outsourcing partners, many of which are located outside of the U.S. The Company has entered into long-term agreements with certain suppliers of flash memory, DRAM and other components, and has made various prepayments associated with supply agreements.

Research and Development: Because the industries in which the Company competes are characterized by rapid technological advances, the Company's ability to compete successfully depends heavily upon its ability to ensure a continual and timely flow of competitive products, services and technologies to the marketplace. The Company continues to develop new technologies to enhance existing products and services, and to expand the range of its offerings through research and development, licensing of intellectual property and acquisition of third-party businesses and technology.

Intellectual Property: The Company currently holds a broad collection of patents, copyrights, trademarks, service marks, trade secrets, and other forms of intellectual property rights relating to certain of its products, services and technologies. The Company has registered or applied for trademarks and service marks in the U.S. and in certain other countries.

Employees: As of September 28, 2024, the Company had approximately 164,000 full-time equivalent employees. The Company considers its relationship with its employees to be good.

Item 1A. Risk Factors

The Company's business, reputation, results of operations, financial condition and stock price can be affected by a number of factors, whether currently known or unknown, including those described below. When any one or more of these risks materialize from time to time, the Company's business, reputation, results of operations, financial condition and stock price can be materially and adversely affected.

Macroeconomic and Industry Risks: The Company's operations and performance depend significantly on global and regional economic conditions and adverse macroeconomic conditions, including inflation, slower growth or recession, new or increased tariffs and other barriers to trade, changes to fiscal and monetary policy, tighter credit, higher interest rates, high unemployment and currency fluctuations, could materially adversely affect demand for the Company's products and services.

Global markets for the Company's products and services are highly competitive and subject to rapid technological change, and the Company may be unable to compete effectively in these markets. The Company faces competition from companies that have significant technical, marketing, distribution and other resources, as well as established hardware, software, digital content and service offerings with large customer bases.

The Company depends on the performance of carriers, wholesalers, retailers and other resellers. The Company depends on telecommunications carriers that sell iPhone, and other resellers such as cellular network carriers, wholesalers, retailers and value-added resellers. If one or more of these resellers were to experience financial difficulties or adverse changes in their business, this could adversely affect the Company.

Product and Service Risks: The Company's products and services may be affected from time to time by design and manufacturing defects that could materially adversely affect the Company's business. The Company offers complex hardware and software products and services that can be affected by design and manufacturing defects. Sophisticated operating system software and applications, as well as cloud-based services, may contain errors, defects and security vulnerabilities.

The Company is exposed to credit risk on its trade accounts receivable, vendor non-trade receivables, and prepayments related to long-term supply agreements, and this risk is heightened during periods when economic conditions worsen. The Company distributes its products and a portion of its services through third-party cellular network carriers, wholesalers, retailers and resellers.

Legal and Regulatory Risks: The Company is subject to complex and changing laws and regulations worldwide, which exposes the Company to potential liabilities, increased costs and other adverse effects on the Company's business. The Company is subject to a variety of laws and regulations in the U.S. and abroad that involve matters central to its business, including, but not limited to, user privacy, rights of publicity, data security, data retention and deletion, intellectual property, content, advertising, consumer protection, accessibility, information security, taxation and e-commerce.

The Company is subject to laws and regulations relating to the handling of personal data, and these laws may change. The Company is subject to a variety of federal, state, local and international laws and regulations regarding privacy, data protection and data security, including those related to the collection, storage, handling, use, disclosure, transfer, and security of personal data. Data protection laws around the world often impose obligations regarding the handling of personal data, including limitations on data collection, retention and use.

The Company's business is subject to the risks of international operations. The Company derives a significant portion of its revenue and earnings from its international operations. Compliance with applicable U.S. and foreign laws and regulations, such as import and export requirements, anti-corruption and anti-bribery laws, tax laws, foreign exchange controls and cash repatriation restrictions, data privacy requirements, and environmental, health and safety laws increases the costs of doing business internationally.

Changes in the Company's effective tax rate could have an adverse effect on the Company's results of operations. The Company is subject to taxes in the U.S. and numerous foreign jurisdictions. Due to economic and political conditions, tax rates in various jurisdictions may be subject to significant change. The Company's effective tax rate could also be affected by changes in the mix of earnings in countries with differing statutory tax rates, changes in the valuation of deferred tax assets and liabilities, and the introduction of new taxes.

Item 1C. Cybersecurity

The Company has processes in place for assessing, identifying, and managing material cybersecurity risks. These processes are integrated into the Company's enterprise risk management program. The Company's cybersecurity organization identifies and assesses risks from cybersecurity threats through a number of mechanisms, including threat intelligence, vulnerability assessments, penetration testing, monitoring of external threat environments, and analysis of reports of threats and actors.

The Company's cybersecurity organization is led by its Chief Information Security Officer, who has over 20 years of experience in information security and technology risk management. The Company invests in training and awareness programs designed to help employees identify and report potential cybersecurity threats.

Item 2. Properties

The Company's headquarters are located in Cupertino, California. As of September 28, 2024, the Company owned or leased facilities and land for corporate functions, R&D, data centers, retail and other purposes in the U.S. and in certain other countries. The Company believes its existing facilities and equipment are in good condition and suitable for the conduct of its business.

Item 3. Legal Proceedings

The Company is subject to legal proceedings and claims that have not been fully resolved and that have arisen in the ordinary course of business. The Company settled certain matters during the period, and the outcome of litigation is inherently uncertain. In the opinion of management, there was not at least a reasonable possibility the Company may have incurred a material loss with respect to loss contingencies for asserted legal and other claims.

Epic Games Litigation: In December 2020, Epic Games filed a lawsuit against the Company in the U.S. District Court alleging antitrust violations related to the App Store. The trial court ruled largely in the Company's favor, and Epic Games appealed. The Supreme Court declined to hear Epic Games' appeal on most issues, and the case has been remanded to the district court for further proceedings regarding anti-steering provisions.

European Commission Investigations: The European Commission has investigated certain of the Company's business practices. In March 2024, the European Commission issued a fine of approximately EUR 1.8 billion, alleging that certain App Store rules constitute unfair trading conditions. The Company has appealed the decision.

Item 7. Management's Discussion and Analysis of Financial Condition and Results of Operations

Fiscal 2024 Overview: Total net revenue was $391.0 billion, an increase of 2% compared to fiscal 2023. The increase was driven primarily by higher Services revenue. Products net revenue was $295.0 billion, a decrease of 1% from fiscal 2023, driven by lower iPhone and iPad revenue, partially offset by higher Mac revenue. Services net revenue was $96.0 billion, an increase of 13% from fiscal 2023, driven by higher revenue from advertising, the App Store, and cloud services.

Gross Margin: Gross margin was $180.7 billion, compared to $169.1 billion in fiscal 2023. Products gross margin increased by $1.2 billion to $112.1 billion, primarily due to cost savings and a different mix of products, partially offset by the different mix of Products revenue. Services gross margin increased by $10.4 billion to $68.6 billion, driven by higher Services net revenue and a favorable mix.

Operating Expenses: Total operating expenses were $57.6 billion, compared to $55.0 billion in fiscal 2023. Research and development expense was $31.4 billion, compared to $29.9 billion in fiscal 2023. This increase was primarily driven by increases in headcount-related expenses. Selling, general and administrative expense was $26.2 billion, compared to $25.1 billion in fiscal 2023, driven by increases in headcount-related expenses and marketing.

Net Income: Net income was $93.7 billion, compared to $97.0 billion in fiscal 2023. Diluted earnings per share was $6.08, compared to $6.13 in fiscal 2023.

Liquidity and Capital Resources: The Company believes its balances of cash, cash equivalents and unrestricted marketable securities, along with cash generated by ongoing operations and continued access to debt markets, will be sufficient to satisfy its cash requirements and capital return program over the next 12 months and beyond.

As of September 28, 2024, the Company had $29.9 billion in cash and cash equivalents. The Company had outstanding fixed-rate notes with varying maturities for an aggregate principal amount of $97.3 billion. During fiscal 2024, the Company returned $110.5 billion to shareholders through dividends and share repurchases.

Capital Return Program: During fiscal 2024, the Company repurchased 545 million shares of its common stock for $95.0 billion. The Company paid dividends of $15.2 billion during fiscal 2024. In May 2024, the Board of Directors authorized an increase of $110.0 billion to the Company's share repurchase program and declared a cash dividend increase of 4%.

Item 7A. Quantitative and Qualitative Disclosures About Market Risk

The Company is exposed to economic risk from foreign currency exchange rates and interest rates. The Company uses derivative instruments to partially offset its business exposure to foreign currency and interest rate risk. The Company may enter into foreign currency forward and option contracts with financial institutions to protect against foreign currency exchange rate risks associated with certain existing assets and liabilities, certain firmly committed transactions, forecasted future cash flows and net investments in foreign subsidiaries.

Interest Rate Risk: The Company's exposure to changes in interest rates relates primarily to its investment portfolio and outstanding debt. The Company places its investments in instruments that meet high credit quality standards and limits the amount of credit exposure to any one issuer.
